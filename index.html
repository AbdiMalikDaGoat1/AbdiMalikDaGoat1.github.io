My Screenprinting Project

An Intro to What This Project Is About?

I grew up in London, and for this project I wanted to make art that actually meant something to me. My name is Abdi, and this is my screen printing project about something I think a lot of people can relate to but never really talk about. The idea is simple. We are so caught up in our own lives that we stop noticing the world around us. This project is my way of saying slow down and look up.

Look Up

The hardest part of this project was not making the art. It was finding something worth saying. I thought about doing something on the Middle East, but that felt too far from my own life. Then one day I just looked around at the people near me, and it clicked. Everyone was distracted. No one was paying attention to where they were. I grew up in London, one of the most beautiful cities in the world, and when I went back years later, people were so caught up in their own things that they were not actually seeing it. That became the idea. I wanted to make art about the lack of appreciation for the places we live in. My first thought was a sunset, but sunsets have too many colors to work with a screen print. So I looked up instead. The sky is something we see every single day but never really think about. That became the focus.

Making It Real

To make the design, I started by drawing the sky in Photoshop using brushes and the paint bucket tool with simple pastel colors. Then I used a DSLR camera to take photos of my own eyes, nose, and mouth. I ran those through the threshold tool to turn them into simple black-and-white drawings with very little detail. After that, I made them white and placed them into the sky as clouds. The idea is that the clouds look almost human; the way they move and shift feels alive. The detail had to be simple because if there is too much, the vinyl rips when it gets cut. Before printing, I planned the layout in Adobe Illustrator. Then I built the screen frame by cutting wood to an outer diameter of 17.5 inches and an inner diameter of 14.5 inches, and stapled four pieces together. After that I stretched the mesh over the frame, cut the vinyl design, and melted it onto the screen. Once it cooled, I mixed the right shade of blue and started printing. It took a couple of tries but the final results came out good.

What I Took Away

This project taught me a lot, but the most valuable thing I walked away with was Photoshop. Before this project I had never really used it. I learned the ins and out of photoshop all within this one project. But what surprised me was that it did not stop there. Learning Photoshop made me want to keep creating things outside of school. I started doing personal art on my own time just because I now had the tool to do it. That is something no other project has done for me. It turned a school assignment into something that actually changed what I do in my free time.

Modern Skill

The 21st century skill I showed the most growth in was Creativity and Innovation. At the start I had a basic idea but it kept not working. The sunset had too many colors. The design was too detailed for the vinyl. Every time something did not work, I had to find a new way. Turning photos of my own face into clouds was not my first idea. It came from hitting a wall and asking what else I could do. That is what the skill is really about. Not just coming up with something original but being willing to change it when it does not work and still end up with something better than what you started with.
