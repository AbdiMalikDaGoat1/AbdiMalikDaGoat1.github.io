My Screenprinting Project

An Intro to What This Project Is About?

I grew up in London, and for this project I wanted to make art that actually meant something to me. My name is Abdi, and this is my screen printing project about something I think a lot of people can relate to but never really talk about. The idea is simple. We are so caught up in our own lives that we stop noticing the world around us. This project is my way of saying slow down and look up.

Look Up

When I started this project, the hardest part was not making the art. It was figuring out what to say with it. I thought about doing something on the Middle East, but that felt too far from my own life. Then one day I just looked around at the people near me and it clicked. Everyone was distracted. No one was paying attention to where they were. I grew up in London, one of the most beautiful cities in the world, and when I went back years later, people were so caught up in their own things that they were not actually seeing it. That became the idea. I wanted to make art about the lack of appreciation for the places we live in. My first thought was a sunset, but sunsets have too many colors to work with a screen print. So I looked up instead. The sky is something we see every single day but never really think about. That became the focus.

Making It Real

To make the design, I started by drawing the sky in Photoshop using brushes and the paint bucket tool with simple pastel colors. Then I used a DSLR camera to take photos of my own eyes, nose, and mouth. I ran those through the threshold tool to turn them into simple black and white drawings with very little detail. After that I made them white and placed them into the sky as clouds. The idea is that the clouds look almost human, the way they move and shift feels alive. The detail had to be simple because if there is too much, the vinyl rips when it gets cut. Before printing, I planned the layout in Adobe Illustrator. Then I built the screen frame by cutting wood to an outer diameter of 17.5 inches and an inner diameter of 14.5 inches, and stapled four pieces together. After that I stretched the mesh over the frame, cut the vinyl design, and melted it onto the screen. Once it cooled, I mixed the right shade of blue and started printing. It took a couple of tries but the final results came out good.
